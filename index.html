Abstract

Recent diffusion-based controllable video generation has achieved motion control for single or multiple objects. However, accurate control with complicated scenarios remains challenging, such as motion control of fine-grained object parts, sophisticated moving trajectories, and coherent background movement. In this paper, we introduce TrackGo, a novel approach that utilizes sparse yet effective point trajectories for enhanced motion control. First, we extract trajectory-based condition features for control points, which are generated from user-input arrows and masked regions. Second, we propose TrackAdapter, an efficient and lightweight adapter that can be easily integrated into existing temporal attention modules. TrackAdapter precisely activates the moving regions of the target object(s) or part(s) within the attention maps, guided by the control inputs. This enables more precise and fine-grained control of movement. Our experiments demonstrate that TrackGo achieves state-of-the-art performance on FVD and FID scores.
